# Bedroom Knockers (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A single doorknocker of polished brass, the heavy ornamental sort that hangs on the front door of a manor house or the townhome of a man with more coin than callers. By day it is exactly that — a knocker, mute and pretty. Its use comes at night, when a wary party makes camp and fixes it to whatever passes for their threshold: a door, a gate, even the flap of a tent. Keyed to its owner and those they name, it becomes a watchful, increasingly unfriendly sentry.

Let a stranger try the door — to open it, slip past it, or break it down — and the knocker speaks, demanding their business while it rouses the sleepers within. Press the matter uninvited and the brass face contorts into a gargoyle's snarl, and the conversation is over.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** An ornate brass doorknocker. *Detect Magic* reveals an aura of abjuration and enchantment; *Identify* names the keying, the escalation, and the once-per-day limit.
- **Placement and keying.** Affixed to any portal — door, gate, tent flap — by its owner. The owner and any persons the owner names are "keyed" and pass freely. Anyone unkeyed who tries to open, pass, or force the portal triggers it.
- **Round one — challenge.** The knocker animates, demands the intruder's reason for entry, and audibly alerts everyone within. If the intruder relents, nothing further happens.
- **Round two — fear.** If the intruder persists without leave, the face becomes a gargoyle's and fires a ray of fear from each of its two eyes at suitable offenders outside the portal. Save vs. spell or flee the area.
- **Round three — sleep.** The mouth speaks words of *sleep*, affecting any still in the area outside the portal. Save vs. spell or sleep 1d4 turns (a sleeper may be roused normally).
- **Round four — binding.** The three horns fire at any remaining targets. Save vs. rod/staff/wand or be magically bound and cuffed where they stand.
- **Limitations.** Usable once per day; once spent, it is an ordinary knocker until the next day. If attacked it has AC 4 and 20 hit points; reduced to 0, it is permanently destroyed. Its effects reach only those *outside* the warded portal.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. Around 5,000–8,000 gp. Prized by adventuring parties who sleep in dangerous places, by caravan masters, and by the merely nervous rich — anyone who wants a door that argues before it bites. A castellan would pay handsomely for one on every guest wing.

## DM Notes

The charm of the Knockers is that the escalation is a clock the intruders can read: the challenge gives fair warning, and a sensible burglar backs off before the eyes light up. The defenders' real advantage is the alert — by round two the party should be awake and arming. Used against the PCs, it is a wonderful alarm-and-delay piece rather than a kill-box.

The original does not say how the knocker chooses "suitable offenders"; rule that the owner's challenge defines intent, so a keyed companion who fumbles the door is not blasted. Note the strict once-per-day cap and the modest 20 hit points: a determined band that eats the fear and weathers a round can simply batter the brass to scrap, which is the intended counterplay.
