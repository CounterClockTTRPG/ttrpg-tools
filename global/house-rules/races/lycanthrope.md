# Lycanthrope (Homebrew Race Template)

> **This is not a standalone race.** Lycanthropy is a condition layered on top of an existing race (human, elf, half-elf, dwarf, halfling, half-orc — never a Large or non-mammalian race). Build the base character first, then apply this template.

## Description

A lycanthrope is a person who can take animal form. The transformation is real — bones reshape, mass redistributes, the human mind retreats to varying depths beneath the animal one. There are two paths to the curse, and they matter:

- **True-born.** Born to lycanthrope parents, or to a parent in animal form at conception. The child grows up knowing what they are, gains control of the change in adolescence, and remembers everything done in animal form. True-borns are statistically rare and almost always isolated from human society.
- **Infected.** Bitten by a lycanthrope, survived the encounter, and failed the save against the curse. The transformation imposes itself — under the full moon, under stress, in the presence of blood — and during the early years the human mind blacks out for the duration. Infected lycanthropes can, with effort and time, learn partial control. Most do not.

A lycanthrope PC must choose one variety (werewolf, werebear, wereboar, weretiger, or wererat — see the table below) and one origin (true-born or infected). The two choices together define what the character can and cannot do.

## Attribute Modifiers

Applied **on top of** the base race's modifiers, in human form:

- Strength +1
- Constitution +1
- Charisma −1 (the curse leaves a faint, unsettling something — animals notice it; sensitive humans notice it; livestock will not let the lycanthrope near them)

In animal or hybrid form, use the form's stats from the table below in place of the human ability scores for STR, DEX, and CON. Mental scores (INT, WIS, CHA) remain unchanged.

## Origin: True-Born vs. Infected

| Property | True-Born | Infected |
|---|---|---|
| **Control of change** | At will | Save vs. polymorph required (see triggers) |
| **Memory in animal form** | Full | None for first 2 years; partial thereafter; full after 5 years of survival |
| **Forced change under full moon** | No | Yes — automatic each night of the full 3 nights |
| **Can be cured?** | No (innate) | Yes, by *remove curse* + *cure disease* + belladonna treatment within 1 hour of first transformation, or by *wish* thereafter |
| **Detected by *detect evil*** | Only if alignment is evil | Always (the curse itself reads as evil) |
| **Pass the curse on by bite** | Yes, in animal form | Yes, in animal form |
| **XP modifier** | −20% in all classes | −10% in all classes |

## The Forms

A lycanthrope has three forms: human (the base race), animal, and hybrid. Switching forms takes one full round, during which the character is at −4 AC and cannot attack. Clothing and worn equipment do not transform — they tear off, drop, or stretch unrecognisably. Magical items remain on the person but only function in human form unless designed otherwise.

| Variety | Animal Form | Hybrid Form (animal/human) | Alignment Pull |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Werewolf** | AC 5, Move 15, bite 2d4 | AC 6, Move 12, bite 2d4 + claw 1d4/1d4 (one grab attempt) | Chaotic Evil |
| **Werebear** | AC 2, Move 9, bite 2d4 + claw 1d3/1d3 + hug 2d8 if both claws hit | AC 4, Move 12, bite 2d4 + claw 2d4/2d4 | Lawful Good |
| **Wereboar** | AC 4, Move 12, gore 2d6 | AC 5, Move 9, gore 2d6 + slam 1d6 | Neutral |
| **Weretiger** | AC 3, Move 12, bite 1d10 + claw 1d6/1d6 (rake on double-hit) | AC 4, Move 12, bite 1d4 + claw 1d6/1d6 | Neutral |
| **Wererat** | AC 6, Move 12, bite 1d3 | AC 6, Move 12, bite 1d3 + weapon | Lawful Evil |

The "alignment pull" is exactly that — a pull, not a chain. Over years, untreated lycanthropy nudges the character's worldview toward the listed alignment. A werewolf PC can be Lawful Good for a long while, but every full moon, something whispers in the other direction.

## Allowed Classes

Apply on top of the base race's class restrictions:

- **All martial classes** are available. The animal forms favour Fighter, Ranger (especially werebear, weretiger), and Barbarian if used.
- **Druid** is fully compatible — many druidic traditions consider lycanthropy a sacred state, particularly werebear and weretiger.
- **Mage and Thief** are available, but the bite of a wererat thief is a classic; weretiger fits a higher-stakes assassin/thief.
- **Cleric / Priest** — most deities of law, healing, or civilization withdraw spell access from a lycanthrope until the curse is cured. A few wild or chaotic gods do not. Talk to your DM. Default ruling: clerics of lawful or good gods lose all spell access while the curse is active; druids and clerics of nature, the moon, or the hunt retain full access.
- **Paladin** — incompatible with infected lycanthropy (the curse violates the oath the moment it manifests). True-born werebear paladins are theoretically possible and exceedingly rare.
- **Bard** — fully compatible.

Maximum levels in any class are reduced by 2 from the base race's allowance, reflecting the disruption the curse causes to long-term study and accumulation of power.

## Special Rules

### Combat

- **Damage Immunity (animal/hybrid form only).** Hit only by silvered weapons or magical weapons of +1 or better. Normal weapons inflict no damage; the wounds close as fast as they open. This does not protect the human form.
- **Natural Weapon Damage.** As listed in the Forms table above. STR bonus to damage applies as normal.
- **Hybrid Grab/Hug/Rake.** Werewolf in hybrid form: if a claw hits, may attempt to hold the target (save vs. paralysis or held; bite at +4 next round). Werebear in animal form: if both claws hit, hug for 2d8. Weretiger in animal form: if both claws hit, two rear-claw rakes for 1d6+1 each.
- **Communication with Kin.** In animal form, can communicate at the level of pack-talk with normal animals of the same species (wolves for werewolves, etc.) and command up to 2d4 such animals as if they were trained.

### Transformation

- **Voluntary change.** True-born: free action once per round. Infected: requires a save vs. polymorph; one attempt per round.
- **Forced change (infected only).** Save vs. polymorph at the listed modifier or transform involuntarily into hybrid form (if asleep or unconscious, animal form). The animal mind takes over for 1d6 turns minimum.
  - Full moon, any of three nights: save at −4. On the night of full moon proper, no save — automatic transformation at moonrise.
  - Tasting fresh blood (own or another's): save at −2.
  - Reduced below 50% hp: save at +0.
  - Extreme stress, sudden fear or rage: save at +2.
- **Reverting at dawn.** Forced transformations end at dawn or after 1d6 turns, whichever is later. Voluntary transformations end when the character chooses.

### Vulnerabilities

- **Silver.** Silvered weapons inflict normal damage and bypass the immunity. A silvered weapon touched to the lycanthrope in any form for 3 consecutive rounds inflicts 1d4 damage per round (as if branded).
- **Wolfsbane.** A sprig held against the skin: save vs. poison or revert to human form immediately and be unable to change for 24 hours. Ingested: 2d4 damage and a 24-hour transformation lockout. Wolfsbane is poisonous to non-lycanthropes too, so this isn't a free option for allies.
- **Belladonna.** A treated dose ingested within 1 hour of being bitten by a lycanthrope: save vs. poison at −2; success means the curse never takes hold. Failure means 1d4 damage and the curse takes hold anyway. Useless after the 1-hour window.

### Social

- **Detection.** Other lycanthropes recognize each other on sight (smell, in animal form). *Detect evil* lights up infected lycanthropes regardless of alignment. *Detect lycanthropy* (1st-level priest spell, if available in the campaign) reveals all lycanthropes within 30 ft.
- **Reaction Penalty.** When the condition is known to NPCs, reaction rolls are at −4 in most settled regions. In rural areas with active lycanthrope folklore, this rises to −6 and may include immediate violence. Some isolated tribes and most druidic circles treat the condition as neutral or even sacred.
- **Cannot enter consecrated ground** of a lawful good or lawful neutral deity without saving vs. spell each round or take 1d4 damage. Werebear true-borns ignore this rule for nature-aligned deities.

### Curing the Curse (infected only)

The standard 2e procedure: cast *cure disease* and *remove curse* on the lycanthrope while they are in human form, ideally within 7 days of infection. After 7 days, also requires a successful save vs. polymorph by the patient (failure means the curse remains). After a year, only a *wish* will cure it.

A wolfsbane decoction prepared by a druid or witch and drunk during the full moon, while bound and watched by allies, is a folk remedy with about a 1-in-6 chance of success and a 5-in-6 chance of doing nothing or worse.

### Languages

No new languages from the template. The lycanthrope can communicate with their animal kin in animal form (see above) but this is not a true language for game purposes.

### Lifespan

Add +20% to the base race's maximum lifespan. The curse slows aging slightly. True-born werebears in particular can live to 200 if not killed.
