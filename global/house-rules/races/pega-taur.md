# Pega-taur (Homebrew PC Race)

*Original creation — a centaur (see `centaur.md`) crossed with a pegasus: a winged horse-folk.*

## Description

Pega-taurs have the torso of a tall, fine-boned humanoid set on the body of a white winged horse — a centaur's silhouette, but lighter through the chest and shoulders, with a great pair of feathered wings folding back along the equine flanks. They are creatures of high mesas, cloud-wreathed plateaus, and the thermals above mountain valleys, and they look down — literally and otherwise — on those bound to the ground. Their tribes are tiny, scattered, and intensely proud; they keep no cities, build nothing they cannot leave behind, and measure wealth in open sky.

A pega-taur PC is an oddity even among their own kind: a wanderer who flew too far, an exile, or one of the rare few curious enough to walk among ground-dwellers and stable their wings under a roof. They make breathtaking scouts and aerial skirmishers and are very nearly useless in a corridor. A wise party plans around the fact that their companion is happiest, and strongest, in the open air — and miserable, cramped, and grounded the moment the ceiling closes in.

## Special Rules

- **Size L (8 ft at the head, ~1,400 lb with wings folded).** As a centaur: cannot enter any space narrower than 5 ft or shorter than 8 ft without squeezing (half move, −2 to attacks), cannot climb ladders, cannot use human furniture/boats/carts, and gear must be custom-built at 4× cost. A folded wingspan makes tight spaces even worse — treat doorways as a squeeze.
- **Flight.** Move 18 on the ground; **Fly 36 (Class C)** aloft. The signature trait — and tightly limited:
  - Cannot fly wearing more than **leather or padded** barding, nor carrying more than 50% of normal load.
  - Needs a running start of ~30 ft and at least **30 ft of clear vertical air**; cannot take off or fly inside most buildings or dungeons.
  - May make a **diving charge**: from at least 100 ft of dive, one lance or spear attack at +2 to hit for double damage, then must pull up. −1 AC for that round.
- **Hooves.** Two hoof attacks (1d6 each) in addition to a weapon attack in melee, exactly as a centaur; may **trample** a Medium-or-smaller creature on a charge (save vs. paralysis or knocked prone for 1d6).
- **Hollow-Boned.** Built for flight: −1 hp per Hit Die rolled (minimum 1/HD), and falls/crushing damage are taken at +1 per die. **Cannot wear metal armour at all** — the weight grounds them permanently and they will not do it.
- **Sky-Born.** No infravision; instead, keen distance vision (spot detail at twice human range in daylight). +1 to all rolls while airborne and in open sky.
- **Grounded Misery.** After one full day with no chance to fly (deep dungeon, sea voyage, dense forest, a cell), suffers −1 to all rolls, worsening by a further −1 each additional day (to a maximum of −3) until they next take wing. A pega-taur kept caged will pine.
- **Woodland/Mountain Stride.** No movement penalty over rough highland terrain, scree, or natural undergrowth.
- **Languages.** Pega-taur, Centaur, Auran, Sylvan, Common. May learn the tongue of giant eagles.
- **Lifespan.** Up to 120 years. Matures at 16.
