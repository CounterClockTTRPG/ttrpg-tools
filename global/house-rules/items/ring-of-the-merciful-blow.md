# Ring of the Merciful Blow (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A plain band of dull pewter-grey metal, unornamented and faintly worn, the sort of ring a pawnbroker would price at a few silver and forget. It carries no gem, no inscription, no obvious craftsmanship. Only in the hand of someone trained to strike from the shadows does it stir, and even then it gives no sign — no glow, no warmth — until the blow lands and the victim folds, breathing, into unconsciousness rather than blood.

The ring was reputedly forged for a guild assassin who tired of killing — or, by a less charitable telling, for a kidnapper who needed his marks delivered intact. Either way the band remembers its purpose. It will not abide a treacherous owner, and it withdraws its favour the moment a sleeping victim is harmed.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** An unadorned grey metal band. *Detect Magic* reveals a faint aura; *identify* or *legend lore* names the property and the restriction.
- **Activation.** The ring functions only when worn by a thief making a backstab attack. A successful backstab that would normally deal damage instead deals no damage and renders the target unconscious. The target receives no saving throw against the effect itself.
- **Duration.** The victim remains unconscious for 1d8 rounds, multiplied by the thief's backstab damage multiplier (x2 at levels 1–4, x3 at 5–8, and so on). The victim may be roused early by allies (a round of vigorous effort) but wakes groggy.
- **The proviso.** If the wearer damages an opponent the ring has knocked out, or stands by while an ally does so, the ring loses all power for one full week. The restriction is on the *helpless* victim only; the wearer may freely re-engage a foe who has roused and stood.
- **Uses.** Three times per day. Spent uses recover at dawn.
- **Limitations.** Works only on a valid backstab — surprise or rear attack against a creature subject to backstab. It has no effect on undead, constructs, or anything immune to sleep or knockout. It does not stack with poisons or other knockout effects; the ring's blow simply replaces lethal damage.

## Rarity and Value

Uncommon. Worth 1,500–2,500 gp to a thief who takes live captures, a bounty hunter, or anyone whose trade rewards mercy or discretion. Of little use to a fighter and openly distrusted by hardened killers, who resent its conscience.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension is the proviso: the ring turns the cleanest takedown in the game into a binding promise of restraint. A party that knocks a guard cold and then cuts his throat to be sure has just disarmed their own best tool for a week — and the ring watches the *ally*, not only the wearer, so the thief must police the whole party's behaviour.

Adjudicate "allows his allies to do so" by intent and opportunity: if the wearer is present and could plausibly intervene, harm done to the sleeper counts. If the thief is dragged off or genuinely unaware, rule in their favour. Note that the multiplier makes high-level backstabs produce long sleeps (x4 = up to 32 rounds), which is generous; treat a captive as recoverable but not safely transportable across great distance without rebinding.
