# Satyr (Homebrew PC Race)

## Description

Satyrs — also called fauns — are five-foot-tall fey with the torso, head, and arms of a man, the legs of a goat, and a pair of black horns curling up through coarse, reddish-brown hair. They live in tolerant disorder among woodland glens, drink heavily, play pipes only they truly know how to use, and are absolutely uninterested in heroism, ambition, or labour. Their natural counterparts are dryads; sages believe satyr-and-dryad pairings produce the next generation of both.

A satyr PC is, on the face of it, a contradiction — an adventuring satyr is a satyr who has chosen to do something tedious. Most are running from a slighted dryad, hunting a stolen instrument, or paying off a wager with the wrong sort of fey lord. They are charming, devious, and surprisingly hard to kill. They never lose a drinking contest with a non-satyr, and most refuse to fight before exhausting at least one round of negotiation, music, or outright bribery. When they do fight, the pipes come out first.

## Special Rules

- **Size M (5 ft).** Standard Medium gear. Cannot wear closed boots — hooves. Most satyrs wear no leg armour at all and rely on Dex.
- **Movement 18.** Faster than a human. Effectively unfatigued in woodlands.
- **Magic Resistance 25%.** Halved from the monstrous-manual version (the full 50% is overkill on a PC). Applied before any save.
- **Horn Butt.** Natural attack, 1d4+1 damage, usable as the satyr's only attack in a round (no off-hand penalty for horns paired with weapon: simply choose).
- **The Pipes.** Each satyr crafts a personal set of pan-pipes; only that satyr can play them properly. Three times per day, the satyr may play one of three songs. All targets within 60 ft must save vs. spell or be affected for 1d6 turns:
  - **Sleep song.** As the *sleep* spell, no level limit. Used on inoffensive intruders the satyr would prefer to rob rather than fight.
  - **Charm song.** As the *charm person* spell, single target chosen by the satyr.
  - **Fear song.** As the *cause fear* spell, all enemies in range.
  - A given target who saves against any one song is immune to all three from this satyr for the rest of the encounter. A bard's countersong nullifies the music in progress.
  - The pipes take a full round to play. The satyr cannot also attack that round. If struck and damaged before the song completes, the song is wasted (the use is not lost — but the round is).
- **Woodland Stealth.** In any natural setting with vegetation, the satyr is 90% undetectable when motionless. Opponents suffer −2 to surprise rolls when a satyr leads the encounter.
- **Infravision 60 ft.**
- **Resistance to Charm and Sleep.** +2 to saves vs. *charm*, *sleep*, *hold*, and any enchantment that targets the will. Satyrs play those tricks; they don't fall for them.
- **Hard Liver.** Effectively immune to ordinary alcohol. Save vs. poison at +4 against ingested poisons. Satyrs are famously, and reliably, the last person standing at any drinking table.
- **Wine-Bound.** A satyr offered a bottle of fine wine (10+ gp value) by a stranger must save vs. spell or be unable to act against the giver for the next hour. The save is at +2 if the satyr already considers the giver an enemy.
- **Languages.** Sylvan, Satyr, Common, Elven. 80% chance to know Centaur if raised near centaurs.
- **Lifespan.** Up to 500 years. Mature at 30. Looks the same at 30 as at 300.
