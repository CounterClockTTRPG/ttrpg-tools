# Mortis Ring (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A Mortis Ring is carved, never cast — turned from ivory as often as from bone, and on principle never worked in metal of any kind. Most are plain to the point of austerity; a few bear cramped engravings along the inner rim, where only the wearer's finger will ever read them. The pallor of the material and the faint chill of it on the skin tell the careful examiner most of what they need to know: this is a thing made to stand between its wearer and the hungrier sorts of death.

While worn, the ring drinks the bite of those attacks that would otherwise leach away a soul's vitality. It cannot do so forever. Each ring is made with a finite reservoir, and when it is spent the ring is merely an old band of bone — protection exhausted, the next chill touch landing in full.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A plain ring of ivory or bone, never metal, sometimes engraved within. *Detect Magic* shows an abjuration aura; *identify* or *legend lore* reveals the warding function and that the ring holds a limited number of charges.
- **Protection.** The wearer is rendered immune to energy drain (level loss) and to ability *drain* (permanent loss of ability points). It does *not* protect against ability *damage* (temporary loss), which lands normally.
- **Charges.** A Mortis Ring holds 50 charges when created and cannot be recharged. Each level of energy drain prevented, and each point of ability drain prevented, costs 1 charge.
- **Example.** A vampire's touch that would drain 2 energy levels instead drains 2 charges from the ring; the wearer loses no levels. A foe that would permanently strip 3 points of Strength costs 3 charges and the wearer keeps their Strength.
- **Exhaustion.** When the reservoir is empty the ring gives no further protection and the next draining attack affects the wearer in full. The ring does not warn of its remaining charges; only *identify* or careful tallying reveals how much ward is left.
- **Limitations.** Protection is automatic and requires no action, but covers only drain effects — it does nothing against ordinary damage, ability damage, instant-death effects, or other curses. One ring protects one wearer; its charges are not shared.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. Worth 7,500–15,000 gp to anyone who expects to face undead, shadow, or the worst of the planar predators — paladins, undead-hunters, and tomb-delvers especially. The price reflects scarcity and the fact that the ring is a consumable: a buyer is paying for 50 charges of survival, after which the band is worth no more than its carving.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension is that the ring is finite, and the wearer rarely knows how finite. A party that treats it as permanent immunity will, sooner or later, walk into a crypt with an empty ring and discover the difference the hard way. Track charges privately and let the dread of an unknown remaining count do the work — a wight here, a spectre there, and the reservoir drains faster than anyone expected.

For grey areas, hold the line between drain and damage: temporary ability *damage* (a shadow's Strength loss, in editions that treat it as temporary; most poisons and many spells) is *not* warded, while permanent drain is. When a single attack mixes drain and ordinary damage, the ring eats only the drain portion. This keeps the ring a specialist's tool rather than a blanket ward, which is exactly the niche its scarcity is meant to fill.
