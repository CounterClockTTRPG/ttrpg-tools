# The Hawkblade (Homebrew Item)

## Description

The blade itself is keen but plain — good steel and nothing more. The artistry is all in the hilt, which is inlaid with cut pieces of jet and malachite in alternating bands, and crowned by a pommel worked into the head of a hawk, beak forward, eyes of polished green stone. A careful hand can draw that hawk's head out from the hilt by roughly half an inch and turn it; it locks into one of three positions, and at each the blade answers, lengthening or drawing in to match.

A faint yellow glow clings to the weapon at all times, bright as a candle held close, so that it cannot easily be carried in secret. Smiths who have handled it suspect it was made for a scout or duellist who valued one familiar grip across every situation — a knife in a crowd, a longsword on the field — and never minded being seen.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A plain blade with a jet-and-malachite hilt and a hawk's-head pommel. *Detect Magic* shows a moderate aura; *identify* reveals the rotating pommel, the three blade-lengths, and the *Speed of the Hawk* power.
- **Changing length.** Pulling the pommel out about half an inch and rotating it to one of three positions reconfigures the blade as a **dagger**, **short sword**, or **longsword**. Changing form is a single action (one round); the chosen form's normal damage applies.
- **Enchantment.** The Hawkblade is +2 to hit in every form but grants *no* bonus to damage.
- **The glow.** It continuously emits a yellow light equivalent to a *light* spell. This cannot be willed off; the only way to quench it is to sheathe the weapon in a scabbard.
- **Speed of the Hawk.** Three times per day the wielder may invoke this power as a free action at the start of a combat round. For that round the wielder strikes first, automatically, with no initiative roll required. If two combatants both invoke a comparable effect in the same round, resolve their actions simultaneously.
- **Limitations.** No bonus damage in any form, and the ever-present light makes stealth difficult unless the blade is sheathed. The form-change requires a free hand on the pommel and cannot be done while the weapon is, for instance, embedded in a foe.

## Rarity and Value

Uncommon. Worth roughly 8,000–12,000 gp. The adjustable length and reliable initiative make it prized by scouts, duellists, and anyone who fights in varied quarters; the unsuppressable glow and lack of damage bonus keep it from commanding a higher price among front-line warriors.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension is the light. *Speed of the Hawk* is a strong, clean benefit — guaranteed initiative three times a day is worth more than the modest +2 — but the weapon announces the wielder in any darkness, ruins ambushes, and cannot be doused except by putting it away, which means giving up the very glow-free moment a thief would want. Players will repeatedly weigh "draw early and lose surprise" against "stay sheathed and lose the initiative power on round one."

Rule *Speed of the Hawk* as overriding the normal initiative roll for the wielder only; allies roll as usual. It does not grant an extra attack, only the first one. If you run side-based initiative, treat the wielder as acting at the very top of the round.
