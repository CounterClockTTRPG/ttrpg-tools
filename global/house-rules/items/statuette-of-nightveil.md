# Statuette of Nightveil (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A small carved figure, plain enough to overlook — which is precisely the trap. The Statuette of Nightveil is identical in every particular to the benign Statuette of Tyadria: the same lines, the same weight, the same command word, even the same magical aura under scrutiny. Set the two side by side and no test short of using one will tell them apart. The Tyadria is a harmless curiosity, a parlour wonder; the Nightveil is a cage.

Speak its command word while holding it and the speaker's soul and consciousness are drawn into the statue, and whatever soul already waits inside takes their place in the body. The displaced wielder slumps unconscious for 1d4 turns, then wakes — in their new shape, fully aware of what has been done to them, and unable to undo it. The swap is one direction only. A body that has once been emptied this way can never use the statue again, so even a sympathetic new occupant cannot simply trade back.

The Nightveil is barely a rumour; only the oldest and wisest sages know it exists at all, which is how it keeps catching out the clever.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A modest carved statuette. *Detect Magic* and *Identify* return results identical to the Statuette of Tyadria and will not distinguish the two — both report the same enchantment aura and command word. Only *legend lore* (or a sage who knows the specific lore) can reveal that a given statuette is the Nightveil rather than its twin.
- **Activation.** A holder who speaks the command word triggers the exchange. There is no saving throw — the act is voluntary, because the victim believes they hold the harmless Tyadria.
- **The exchange.** The speaker's soul enters the statue; the soul already housed within takes the now-vacant body. The dispossessed wielder (now inside the figure) falls unconscious for 1d4 turns, then wakes, fully cognizant.
- **One use per body.** Once a given body has triggered the statue, it will never function for that body again. The swap cannot be reversed by repeating the command word, however willing the new occupant.
- **Limitations.** Only a replacement soul can free the trapped one — the statue must be fed another consciousness to release the current prisoner. Nothing short of direct divine intervention can remove a soul with none to take its place.

## Rarity and Value

Very rare. As a captured-soul prison it has no honest market; a corrupt collector, cult, or vengeful archmage might pay 10,000 gp or far more, while most who grasp its nature would sooner see it destroyed (or quietly mislabelled and gifted to an enemy). Its true worth is as a weapon of betrayal, not a treasure.

## DM Notes

The whole point is the deception: the players should encounter the Nightveil believing it to be a Tyadria, ideally after handling a genuine Tyadria so the swap feels safe. The horror lands when a trusted item turns the key. Use it sparingly and never as a gotcha without an out — the trapped PC is *aware* and can be rescued, which keeps a swapped player engaged rather than benched.

Mind the 10% loaded chamber: roll (or pre-decide) whether this particular Nightveil holds an ordinary trapped soul or a powerful evil extraplanar creature waiting to be loosed on the region. Decide that before play, because it shapes the whole arc — a freed fiend is a campaign event, not a scene. The escape clause (a replacement soul) is the quest hook: someone must be willing, tricked, or sacrificed to set the prisoner free.
