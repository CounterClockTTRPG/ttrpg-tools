# Objects of Obsessiveness (Homebrew Item)

## Description

They take almost any humble form: a painted ceramic plate, a small statuette, a poorly painted card from some forgotten deck, a cheaply bound folio of illuminated short stories. Individually they are worthless — the sort of thing that gathers dust on a junk peddler's back shelf. The curse hides in the handling. The instant a creature first touches one, a memory surfaces that was never theirs: a half-recalled tale of a *set*, a complete collection that, once gathered, will prove either fabulously valuable or will spell out the clues to some buried mystery of power.

The conviction is total and entirely manufactured. The afflicted will swear they heard of the collection years ago and simply forgot. From that moment they hunt the missing pieces — haunting bazaars and trinket shops, badgering oracles, consulting mystics and wise men, chasing any rumor, poring over dusty tomes — certain that the next acquisition completes the whole. It never does.

No one knows who first seeded these objects, or why. Most who have studied the phenomenon conclude there is no collection at all, only the certainty that there is.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A mundane trinket of any kind, showing no craftsmanship of note. *Detect Magic* reveals a faint enchantment/charm aura; *identify* or *legend lore* names the object as cursed and describes the obsession but cannot enumerate any "set," because none exists.
- **The compulsion.** The first creature to touch an object with bare skin is affected immediately; no saving throw is permitted, as with most cursed items. The victim becomes convinced the object is one piece of a larger collection of great value or arcane significance, and is driven to seek the rest.
- **Behaviour.** The afflicted will reorder their priorities to pursue the collection: spending coin freely on likely "pieces," diverting travel, neglecting other obligations, and following any lead however thin. They remain otherwise themselves and are not suicidal, but they will argue, bargain, and scheme to continue the hunt.
- **Ending it.** Only *remove curse* (cast by a priest or wizard of level equal to or greater than the victim) or a *limited wish* / *wish* dispels the conviction. Mundane reasoning, evidence, or even confronting the victim with a duplicate object will not break it.
- **Limitations.** The curse compels searching, not violence or theft beyond the victim's normal disposition. Once cured, the victim feels foolish but suffers no lasting penalty. A given object affects one bearer at a time; passed to another, the new holder is afflicted afresh.

## Rarity and Value

Uncommon as a phenomenon, though any single object appears worthless (1–5 gp as junk). The true "value" is to whoever planted it — a rival, a cult, or a long-dead trickster — as a tool for steering a victim's wanderings. No honest merchant prices the curse, because no honest merchant admits to it.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension is that the collection is a fiction, so the hunt can never end on its own. This makes the item a superb adventure engine: the afflicted character supplies their own motivation, and the DM can dangle "pieces" anywhere a plot hook is wanted. Resist the urge to actually assemble a rewarding set — the payoff is meant to recede forever, and revealing a real treasure undercuts the curse.

Adjudicate the compulsion as strong suggestion, not puppetry: let the player roleplay the obsession and only override choices that would abandon the search entirely. If the party wants the curse gone cheaply, remind them *remove curse* is the clean answer — the drama lies in whether they notice the affliction before it has dragged them somewhere costly.
