# Witch (Homebrew Class)

## Description

The witch is the village's other magic-user — the one who is not invited to court, not paid by the temple, and not entirely trusted by either. Where wizards work from books and priests from faith, witches work from pacts. Every witch has a patron: a hag in a swamp, an old fey lord in a stone circle, a forgotten goddess, a demon who keeps a list. The patron grants spells, teaches hexes, and asks favours in return — small ones at first, larger as the witch grows in power. Most witches die in their beds at a great age. The ones who don't tend to die in spectacular ways.

A witch's signature ability is the **named hex**: a personal, sustained curse laid on a single living target, which follows that target wherever they go until the witch sleeps, dies, or chooses to release it. Witches who lean into this ability often don't sleep at all when there's work to be done. There are stories of witches who haven't slept in years.

## Class Requirements

| Ability | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Intelligence | 12 |
| Wisdom | 12 |
| Charisma | 13 |

Prime requisites: **Charisma and Wisdom**. A witch with 16+ in both gains the standard 10% XP bonus.

**Allowed alignments.** Any non-Lawful Good. Most witches are Neutral or Chaotic of some flavour; Lawful Evil witches exist (the contract-bound kind). The pact itself rules out Lawful Good — patrons of that alignment do not grant hexes.

**Allowed races.** Human, half-elf, half-orc, gnome (illusionist tradition), kobold (homebrew), satyr (homebrew). Elves and dwarves traditionally do not study the witch's path; their magical traditions consider it ungoverned and dangerous.

## Mechanics

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Hit Die | d4 |
| THAC0 progression | As priest (20 at 1st, 18 at 4th, 16 at 7th, ...) |
| Saving throws | As mage |
| XP table | As mage |
| Maximum level | Unlimited for humans; demihuman limits as wizard table −1 level |

**Allowed weapons.** Dagger, staff, sickle, dart, sling, knife. A witch may also use any weapon the patron specifically permits (talk to your DM — a hag-patroned witch might use a bone-axe; a fey-patroned witch a longbow).

**Allowed armour.** None. A witch in any armour, or carrying a shield, cannot cast spells, use lesser hexes, or maintain the named hex.

**Weapon proficiencies.** 2 at 1st level, +1 every 4 levels. Non-proficiency penalty −3.
**Non-weapon proficiencies.** 4 at 1st level, +1 every 3 levels. Suggested: Herbalism, Healing, Reading/Writing, Spellcraft, Local History, Animal Lore.

## Spellcasting

A witch casts from a custom **witch spell list** (see below) prepared each morning from her **grimoire** — a personal hand-bound book that doubles as a hex-totem and contains every spell she has ever learned. A witch begins play knowing all 1st-level witch spells; higher-level spells are added as she gains levels (one new spell per new level reached, plus any she finds in another witch's grimoire and successfully copies).

Spell slots per day match the priest table for spells of level 1–4 only; the witch never gains 5th-level or higher spell slots. (Higher-level effects come from major hexes, not spells.)

| Witch level | 1st | 2nd | 3rd | 4th |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | – | – | – |
| 2 | 2 | – | – | – |
| 3 | 2 | 1 | – | – |
| 5 | 3 | 2 | 1 | – |
| 7 | 3 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
| 9 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 2 |
| 11 | 4 | 4 | 3 | 3 |
| 13 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 3 |
| 15+ | 5 | 5 | 4 | 4 |

Bonus spells from high Wisdom apply as for a priest.

**If the grimoire is destroyed**, the witch retains the named hex and her lesser hexes but loses all spellcasting until she scribes a new one (1 month of work, 500 gp in materials). A witch's grimoire is therefore the primary thing her enemies try to burn.

### Witch Spell List (sample)

This is illustrative; the list draws from priest, wizard, and patron-specific spells, themed toward curse, charm, divination, illusion, and small-scale nature magic. Tune to taste.

- **1st** — Bless (reversed: Bane), Cause Light Wounds, Charm Person, Detect Magic, Faerie Fire, Light (rev: Darkness), Sleep, Spook, Ventriloquism
- **2nd** — Augury, Charm Person or Mammal, ESP, Forget, Hold Person, Invisibility, Knock, Pyrotechnics, Stinking Cloud, Web
- **3rd** — Bestow Curse, Clairvoyance, Dispel Magic, Hold Animal, Speak with Dead, Summon Insects, Tongues, Vampiric Touch
- **4th** — Charm Monster, Confusion, Divination, Fear, Polymorph Self, Reincarnate, Wall of Fog, Wither

## Hexes

A witch has three categories of hex: **lesser hexes** (cheap, frequent), the **named hex** (the signature ability), and **major hexes** (rare, dramatic). All hexes require a free hand and the witch's voice; gagging or binding both hands disables them.

### Lesser Hexes

Lesser hexes are short spell-likes that do not consume spell slots. The witch knows two lesser hexes at 1st level, learns a new one every odd level (3, 5, 7, ...), and may use a total number per day equal to **3 + Wisdom bonus + half witch level (round down)**.

Each lesser hex requires one round to deliver, line of sight, and (where applicable) a save vs. spell to negate.

Examples (the witch picks her starter set; new ones available on level-up):

- **Evil Eye.** Target within 60 ft saves vs. spell or suffers −2 to attack rolls and saves for 3 rounds.
- **Ill Omen.** Target within 60 ft saves vs. spell or has their next d20 roll within 1 turn made twice and the worse result kept.
- **Knot Tongue.** Target within 30 ft saves vs. spell or cannot speak intelligibly for 1 round per witch level. No verbal-component spells, no commands, no warning shouts.
- **Sour the Milk.** Touch range. Spoils food, curdles drink, taints small quantities of water. Used to ruin a feast; used to poison nothing — this is not lethal, just nasty.
- **Withering Touch.** Touch attack roll required. 1d4 damage and target saves vs. spell or suffers −1 STR for 1 turn.
- **Crooked Step.** Target within 30 ft saves vs. paralysis or stumbles, prone for 1 round.
- **Whispering Wind.** Carries 25 words to any one named individual within 1 mile, in a voice only they can hear. Often used to taunt.

### Named Hex (Signature Ability)

This is the heart of the class.

**At 1st level**, the witch may name **one** living target as her hexed enemy. The naming takes one full round and requires either:
- the target's **true name** (which the witch must have learned beforehand), **or**
- the witch and target to have **stood within 10 ft of each other and made direct eye contact**, at any point in the witch's life. Eye contact through scrying or a mirror does not count.

The named target makes a save vs. spell at the moment of naming. If they save, they are immune to this witch's named hex for 24 hours and the use is wasted. If they fail, the hex takes hold.

**While hexed**, the target suffers persistent penalties chosen by the witch at naming, drawn from the table below according to witch level:

| Witch level | Hex penalty (witch chooses) |
|---|---|
| 1–3 | −1 to attack rolls, **or** −1 to damage, **or** −1 to saves, **or** +1 to AC of those attacking them |
| 4–6 | −1 to attack **and** −1 to damage, **or** −2 to any single category above |
| 7–9 | −2 to attack **and** −2 to damage, **or** −3 to a single category, **or** target loses 1 hp per turn (not round) |
| 10–12 | As level 7–9, plus the target's healing from non-magical rest is halved |
| 13+ | As above, plus the witch may use any of her own lesser hexes against the target with no save and at no daily cost |

The penalties cap at −2/−2 even if multiple effects would stack — that is the design ceiling.

**The hex ends when:**
- the witch falls asleep, dies, or falls unconscious for more than one round
- the target dies, leaves the witch's plane, or crosses more than 100 miles of open sea
- a *remove curse* spell is cast successfully against the witch's level (witch's level vs. caster's level — opposed roll)
- the witch chooses to release the hex (free action)
- the witch names a new target, ending the prior hex

**Number of simultaneous hexes:** one at 1st–6th level. A second is added at 7th, a third at 13th. The witch may choose the same target for multiple slots only at 13+, and the penalties from the same witch's hexes do not stack (this is the cap).

**Sleep penalty.** The classic witch-on-vendetta trope. The witch may force herself to remain awake to keep a hex active — at a cost. Each consecutive day without sleep imposes a cumulative −1 to attacks, saves, and spell rolls (max −4 at four days). At five days, save vs. paralysis each morning or collapse for 1d6 hours (ending the hex). The witch's body breaks before the hex does.

### Major Hexes

Major hexes are large, slow, dramatic effects. The witch learns one major hex at 5th level and one more at every fourth level thereafter (9, 13, 17, ...). Each major hex is usable **once per week**.

Sample major hexes:

- **Wasting.** Target within line of sight saves vs. death magic or contracts a wasting disease: −1 CON per week, no natural healing, until cured by *cure disease* + *remove curse*. Death at CON 3.
- **Ill Weather.** Centred on the witch, 1-mile radius, 1d4 days. Heavy rain, fog, or unseasonable cold of the witch's choosing. No save (it's weather). Travel halved, ranged attacks at −2.
- **Compelling Pact.** Target within 30 ft saves vs. spell at −2 or is bound to a single oath of the witch's wording for 1 month. Breaking the oath inflicts 4d6 damage and lifts the compulsion. The oath must be specific and achievable.
- **The Long Sleep.** As the *sleep* spell, no level limit, 60-ft radius from the witch, sleeps until dawn or until shaken awake by an ally already awake. Witch loses 1 CON for 24 hours after casting.
- **Black Goat's Visit.** Calls on the patron directly. The DM rolls 1d6: 1–2 the patron is busy, hex fails; 3–4 a minor servitor arrives and performs one task; 5–6 the patron answers in person, briefly. Use sparingly; the patron remembers being called.

## Familiar

A witch gains a familiar at 1st level at no XP cost. Choose from cat, raven, toad, owl, hare, ferret, or fox; rarer options (snake, bat, weasel, pig, goat) at DM discretion.

- The familiar is more intelligent than a normal animal of its kind (INT 6) and understands one language the witch speaks.
- The witch may, by concentrating one round, see and hear through the familiar's senses for up to 1 turn, at any distance up to 1 mile.
- The familiar grants the witch +1 to a single save category permanently (chosen at binding), reflecting its area of mystical specialty (cat: spell; raven: rsw; toad: poison; owl: paralysis; etc.).
- **If the familiar dies**, the witch immediately suffers 2d4 damage and −2 CON for 1 month. A new familiar may be bound after the month is out, in a 24-hour ritual costing 100 gp per witch level.
- The familiar has 1+1 HD, the natural attacks of its species, and shares half the witch's hp pool while within 60 ft (damage to the familiar reduces the witch's hp first; this is how the bond protects the small fragile creature).

## Brewing

A witch may brew certain potions, salves, and minor poisons without the laboratory and time costs of a full alchemist:

- **From 1st level:** healing salve (heals 1d4, 50 gp, 1 day to brew), sleep draught (as the spell when ingested, save vs. poison at −2, 100 gp, 2 days), minor venom (1d4 damage on weapon, single use, save vs. poison negates, 50 gp, 1 day).
- **From 5th level:** love philtre (charm person on willing drinker who knows what it is, otherwise unwilling drinker saves at +2; 500 gp, 1 week), tincture of seeing (clairvoyance for 1 hour; 750 gp, 1 week), hex-binding wax (focus for the named hex; if shaped with a strand of the target's hair, the witch may name them at any distance; 250 gp, 3 days, single use).
- **From 9th level:** flight ointment (rubbed on a broom, gives flight as the spell for 1 hour; 1,500 gp, 2 weeks), wasting tincture (induces the Wasting major hex if ingested; 2,000 gp, 1 week, single use).

Brewing requires Herbalism proficiency and access to fresh ingredients from a wilderness or marketplace.

## The Patron

Every witch has a patron, named in session zero. The patron is a roleplay anchor with a few mechanical hooks:

- **Spell flavour.** The witch's grimoire entries are written in the patron's idiom (a hag's spells smell of swamp; a fey lord's spells flicker silver; a devil's spells are written in tight legal script).
- **Bonus lesser hex.** The patron grants one extra lesser hex at 1st level, themed to their nature. Examples: a hag patron grants **Curdle** (sour milk extended to wells); a fey lord grants **Glamour** (illusory minor disguise on self for 1 turn); a devil grants **The Bargain** (target may save vs. spell or be compelled to truthfully answer one yes/no question).
- **Favours owed.** The DM tracks favours. Roughly once per level gained, the patron asks for something specific — an item retrieved, a person spared, a place visited. Refusing a favour costs the witch 1 level of ability (no spells, no hexes) until the favour is performed or atoned for.
- **Patronage withdrawn.** A witch who repeatedly defies her patron, or whose patron is destroyed, loses her named hex first, then her major hexes, then her spellcasting. The lesser hexes remain (those are *her* knowledge, not the patron's grant). Recovery requires either reconciliation or a new patron, both of which are full adventures in themselves.

## Restrictions and Drawbacks

- **Reaction Penalty.** In settled, religious, or rural communities where witches are feared, reaction rolls are at −2 to −4 once the witch's nature is known. In cosmopolitan cities and many fey-tolerant cultures, this penalty is reduced or absent.
- **Detection.** *Detect evil* lights up most witches (the patron's mark, regardless of the witch's own alignment). *Detect magic* on the witch's grimoire shows it as strongly enchanted permanently.
- **Cannot wear armour or use shields** while casting or hexing — already noted, repeated for emphasis.
- **No turning undead, no holy symbols, no priestly access to deities.** A witch is not a priest; her god, if she has one, does not answer prayers in the priestly sense.
- **Cannot cast in consecrated temples** of lawful good or lawful neutral deities. The space rejects her magic — spells fizzle, hexes fail to take hold, lesser hexes are still usable but at one full extra round of casting time.
