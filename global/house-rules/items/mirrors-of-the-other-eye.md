# Mirrors of the Other Eye (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A matched pair of pocket mirrors, each roughly the size of a playing card, backed in tarnished silver and framed in a thin band of dark pewter. The glass is flawless. Engraved on the back of each, in a cramped but legible hand, is a single word — the same word on both, but mirrored: one reads left-to-right, the other right-to-left, as if one were the reflection of the other.

When neither is activated they behave as ordinary mirrors. When the command word is spoken aloud while holding one, its surface clouds briefly and then resolves into whatever the other mirror currently faces — its precise field of view, as if the holder were standing behind it looking through it. The image is silent; there is no sound channel. The mirror shows what it *faces*, not what its holder is looking at.

They were made, by most accounts, for a pair of siblings who spent their lives on opposite sides of a very old feud and wanted a way to check, occasionally, that the other was still alive.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** Matched pair; identical in every respect except the mirrored engraving. *Detect Magic* reveals a divination aura. *Identify* names the property and the command word.
- **Activation.** Speaking the command word while holding a mirror activates the viewing. The mirror shows what the *other* mirror currently faces — its field of view (roughly a 90° cone from the reflective surface outward, to a distance of 30 feet). Duration: concentration, up to 10 minutes. The holder may end it early by speaking the command word again or setting the mirror face-down.
- **The image.** Visual only, no sound. Same resolution and light conditions as the remote location — if the other mirror is in a dark room, the image is dark. If it faces a wall, the image is wall. The image does not move unless the mirror moves.
- **Both activated at once.** If both mirrors are activated simultaneously, each shows the other's view. Each holder sees through the other's mirror. This is the intended use.
- **Passive exposure.** A mirror that is face-up and unattended is always *showing* — anyone who activates the other mirror sees whatever the passive one faces. Leaving a mirror on a table face-up is leaving a window open.
- **Blocking.** Covering the mirror face (cloth, pocket, held face-down) blocks the image entirely. The receiving mirror shows nothing, or goes dark if it was showing an image. Blocking does not end the activation — the holder still needs to speak the command word or wait out the duration.
- **Range.** Unlimited distance, same plane. Across planes: the mirrors cloud and show nothing.
- **Detection.** A *detect magic* cast in the vicinity of an active mirror reveals it as a scrying sensor. *Nondetection* on the mirror suppresses the aura but does not block the view.
- **Limitations.** The mirrors show only what is visible — no infravision through the image, no magical sight (true seeing, detect invisible) unless the viewer possesses that ability independently and is looking through their own activated mirror. They cannot be used to scry on specific creatures or locations; they show what the other mirror faces, no more.

## Rarity and Value

Uncommon. Worth 300–500 gp as a matched pair; each mirror alone is worth very little (perhaps 30 gp as a curiosity). They are not useful separated unless the goal is leaving a window open somewhere.

## DM Notes

The key tension is the passive-exposure rule. Players will eventually leave a mirror somewhere and forget to cover it, or hand one to an NPC and not think about what the NPC can see. The mirrors reward attentive play and punish inattention equally.

For the twins theme: the mirrors work best when the two holders are operating in different locations and need to coordinate silently — one scouts, the other reads the image and signals the group. The 10-minute duration is generous but not unlimited; prolonged surveillance requires someone to keep the command word running.

Sound being absent is deliberate. The items do not replace communication; they supplement it. Two characters who can lip-read have a significant advantage.
