# Staff of Tripping (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A long, supple staff of pale springy wood, unremarkable to the eye save for a faint suppleness in the grain, as though the whole length were under tension and waiting to spring. It fights well enough as a quarterstaff. Its real trick reveals itself only on a clean strike, when the staff seems to find a will of its own — whipping down and round to coil about an enemy's ankles like a living thing, and dragging them off their feet.

No grand provenance attends it; it is the sort of practical, slightly mischievous item that turns up in a hedge-wizard's kit or a wandering monk's pack. Those who have used one tend to keep it.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A plain pale quarterstaff with an unusual springiness to the wood. *Detect Magic* reveals a faint alteration aura; *identify* or *legend lore* names the +1 enchantment and the tripping property.
- **Weapon.** Attacks as a staff of +1 enchantment (+1 to attack and damage).
- **The trip.** On a natural attack roll of 19 or 20, instead of dealing damage the staff whips around the target's ankles. The target is entitled to a saving throw vs. rod/staff/wand to avoid being thrown down.
  - **On a failed save:** the target is tripped, falls prone, and is subject to the rules for prone/flat-footed subjects. The staff then releases.
  - **On a successful save:** the target keeps their feet *that round*, but the staff remains coiled fast about their ankles. To pull free by force requires a successful bend bars/lift gates roll. Otherwise, the held target must make a new successful save vs. rod/staff/wand each round or be tripped that round; a failed save trips them (prone, and the staff releases).
- **Limitations.** The trip works only against creatures with legs that the staff can plausibly encircle — no effect on the legless, the gaseous, or creatures more than one size category larger than the wielder (DM's discretion). While the staff is coiled about a held target, the wielder cannot use it to strike another foe.

## Rarity and Value

Uncommon. Worth 2,000–4,000 gp to a fighter or monk who likes to control a battlefield, or to anyone who prefers taking foes alive and grounded rather than dead. The narrow trigger (19–20) keeps it from being a reliable lockdown, which keeps the price modest.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension sits in the *held* state on a successful save: the target is on their feet but tethered, the wielder is committed to holding them, and both sides are now spending actions on the contest rather than on the rest of the fight. That is a small, vivid duel inside the larger battle, and it rewards a wielder who picks the right target to pin.

Adjudicate the "held" round-by-round: the target may attack, cast, or attempt to flee, but movement away is impossible until they break free by bend bars/lift gates or the staff trips them loose. Where the original was terse, I have ruled the wielder's staff occupied while a target is held, so locking down a foe genuinely costs the wielder their own offense — that cost is what keeps the 19–20 trip fair.
