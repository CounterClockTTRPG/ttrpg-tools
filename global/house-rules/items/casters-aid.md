# Caster's Aid (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A modest signet-style band favoured by wizards who hate being caught with a spell half-readied. Into it a caster may "pour" a prepared spell — speaking it as if to cast, but releasing the magic into the ring rather than the world. The spell leaves the caster's mind exactly as a cast spell would, yet produces no effect; it waits, coiled, in the ring. A later word of command looses it, fully formed, on the terms it was first shaped with.

The ring's worth is timing. A mage can lock in a *fireball*'s direction and range in the safety of a back room, scrub it from memory to free attention, and then, rounds later, speak a single word and have it strike. The unwary, however, learn that the ring is a poor judge of which thought to seize, and a careless command can cost a spell the caster never meant to spend.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A plain band of no obvious make. *Detect Magic* shows an alteration aura; *identify* or *legend lore* reveals the store-and-release function, the command word, and the Intelligence-related risk below.
- **Storing a spell.** The wearer casts a spell *into* the ring instead of into the world. The spell is removed from memory as a normal casting would remove it, but has no immediate effect. All components and conditions must be satisfied at this moment: material components are consumed, and the spell's area (if not fixed), direction, and target are locked in now.
- **Release window.** From the round after storing, and for a number of rounds equal to the caster's Intelligence score, the wearer may speak the ring's command word. Two segments later the stored spell discharges with exactly the parameters set when it was stored.
- **Expiry.** If the window lapses without the command word being spoken, the stored spell dissipates and is lost.
- **The Intelligence risk.** A caster of Intelligence 14 or lower must make a saving throw vs. spell when they speak the command word. On a failure, the ring's power tears a *random* memorized spell from the wearer's mind in addition to releasing the stored one. Casters of Intelligence 15+ are exempt.
- **Limitations.** The ring holds one spell at a time and cannot store a spell whose targeting cannot be fixed at the moment of storing. It confers no extra spell slots and saves no casting time at the moment of storage — only at the moment of need. The stored spell's saving throws, ranges, and durations are those of the original casting.

## Rarity and Value

Uncommon. Worth 2,500–5,000 gp to any wizard who fights on others' schedules — battle-mages, ambushers, and bodyguards prize a held *fireball* or *hold person*. The Intelligence penalty makes it markedly less attractive to lower-Intelligence casters, which in practice means most buyers are exactly the high-Intelligence specialists who can ignore the drawback.

## DM Notes

The fun here is pre-loading: a player who plans ahead gets a devastating "instant" spell, paying for it with a slot spent early and the gamble of the release window. Hold them to the parameters fixed at storage — a *fireball* aimed at a doorway cannot be re-aimed when the enemy comes through a window. That constraint is the balancing cost; do not let players treat the ring as a free delayed-targeting upgrade.

Adjudicate the Intelligence save plainly: roll it openly when a low-Intelligence wearer triggers the ring, and determine the lost spell randomly from those currently memorized. The threat is meant to discourage the very casters least able to afford a wasted slot, so resist softening it — the risk is the reason the high-Intelligence market exists.
