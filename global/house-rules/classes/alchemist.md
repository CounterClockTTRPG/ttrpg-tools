# Alchemist (Homebrew Class)

## Description

The alchemist is what happens when a wizard decides spells are too unreliable, too long to memorise, and impossible to lend to a friend. Instead of memorising patterns and discharging them through gestures and syllables, the alchemist distils effects into glass: vials of liquid that explode, restore, transmute, or illuminate when uncorked. A fighter can throw an alchemist's bomb. A thief can drink an alchemist's potion. The alchemist's magic, such as it is, lives in the bottle — and runs out when the bottle is empty.

A working alchemist is part scholar, part chemist, part field medic, and part demolitionist. They smell of sulphur and pine resin. They tend to be paranoid about open flame around their packs. They are routinely accused of being necromancers, poisoners, and devil-worshippers, often by people who have never seen a glass alembic before. Most alchemists take the accusation as part of the job.

## Class Requirements

| Ability | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Intelligence | 13 |
| Dexterity | 12 |
| Wisdom | 9 |

Prime requisite: **Intelligence**. INT 16+ grants the standard 10% XP bonus.

**Allowed alignments.** Any. The class is materialist, not aligned. Lawful alchemists keep meticulous lab notebooks; chaotic alchemists experiment on themselves. Both die in roughly equal numbers.

**Allowed races.** Human, half-elf, dwarf, gnome (especially), halfling, half-orc, kobold (homebrew), doppelganger (homebrew). Gnomes get a bonus to formula learning (see below). Elves rarely take the class — they consider the discipline crude — but it is permitted.

## Mechanics

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Hit Die | d6 |
| THAC0 progression | As thief (20 at 1st, 19 at 3rd, then +1 per 2 levels) |
| Saving throws | As mage |
| XP table | As thief |
| Maximum level | Unlimited for humans; demihuman limits as wizard +1 (gnomes are unrestricted in the alchemist class) |

**Allowed weapons.** Dagger, dart, sling, hand crossbow, light crossbow, club, staff. The bomb is treated as a separate attack form (see below) and is not a weapon proficiency.

**Allowed armour.** Padded, leather, studded leather. No shield while throwing bombs or brewing in the field. Heavier armours interfere with the fine motor work the class depends on.

**Weapon proficiencies.** 2 at 1st, +1 every 4 levels. Non-proficiency penalty −3.
**Non-weapon proficiencies.** 5 at 1st level (one of which must be Herbalism), +1 every 2 levels. Suggested: Healing, Reading/Writing, Spellcraft, Appraising, Ancient History, Mining, Navigation, Pottery (for sealing vials).

## Core Mechanic: Brewing

The alchemist's daily power lives in three forms: **extracts** (drinkable potions), **bombs** (thrown vials), and **salves** (applied unguents). All three are consumable: when used, they're gone.

Each morning, after a full night's rest and one hour of brewing at dawn, the alchemist prepares a daily batch:

| Alchemist level | Extracts/day | Bombs/day | Salves/day |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | 2 + INT bonus | 1 |
| 2 | 2 | 3 + INT bonus | 1 |
| 3 | 2 + 1 of 2nd rank | 4 + INT bonus | 2 |
| 5 | 4, including 2nd rank | 6 + INT bonus | 2 |
| 7 | 5, including 3rd rank | 8 + INT bonus | 3 |
| 9 | 6, including 4th rank | 10 + INT bonus | 3 |
| 11 | 7, including 5th rank | 12 + INT bonus | 4 |
| 13+ | +1 every 2 levels | +1 every level | +1 every 3 levels |

The alchemist may distribute the extract count across any ranks they have access to (a 5th-level alchemist with 4 extracts could prep 2 of 1st rank and 2 of 2nd, or 4 of 1st, etc.).

**Brewed consumables go inert after 24 hours**, regardless of how they are stored. This is a hard cap — the alchemist cannot stockpile a week's worth of bombs the way a wizard cannot stockpile a week's worth of memorised spells. Anything not used by the next dawn is poured out and re-brewed.

**Brewing requires the alchemist's lab kit** (5 lb, 50 gp, carried in a sturdy case). Without the kit, no brewing. A destroyed kit costs 50 gp and three days to replace at any major town. A custom kit (engraved, gnome-made, etc.) is a meaningful character item.

## Extracts

Extracts are single-use vials of distilled effect. The drinker — alchemist or anyone else — receives the effect as if it had been cast on them. Extracts mirror priest and wizard spells, but only the **personal-target subset**: anything that affects the caster's own body, senses, or close space. Extracts cannot be used to fireball a room or charm a duke; those go in bombs and salves respectively.

| Rank | Roughly equivalent to | Available at level |
|---|---|---|
| 1st rank | 1st-level personal spells | 1 |
| 2nd rank | 2nd-level personal spells | 3 |
| 3rd rank | 3rd-level personal spells | 5 |
| 4th rank | 4th-level personal spells | 7 |
| 5th rank | 5th-level personal spells | 9 |
| 6th rank | 6th-level personal spells (rare) | 13 |

### Sample Extracts

- **1st rank** — Cure Light Wounds, Detect Magic, Detect Poison, Endure Cold/Heat, Enlarge (self), Feather Fall, Jump, Light, Pass Without Trace, Protection from Evil, Resist Fire/Cold, Shield, Spider Climb
- **2nd rank** — Bull's Strength (+1d4 STR for 1 hour), Cat's Grace (+1d4 DEX), Cure Moderate Wounds, Darkvision, Detect Invisible, Invisibility (self), Levitate, Mirror Image, See in Darkness, Web (cocoon self for cover)
- **3rd rank** — Cure Serious Wounds, Fly, Gaseous Form, Haste (self only), Heroism, Non-detection, Tongues, Water Breathing
- **4th rank** — Cure Critical Wounds, Free Action, Polymorph Self, Restoration (lesser), Stoneskin
- **5th rank** — Heal, Mind Blank, True Seeing
- **6th rank** — Regenerate, Shapechange (single shape, prepared)

Drinking an extract takes one round and provokes attacks of opportunity (or the 2e equivalent — interruption disrupts it as a spell cast in melee). Pouring an extract down an unconscious ally's throat takes two rounds.

## Bombs

Bombs are the alchemist's primary combat tool. A bomb is a stoppered vial of unstable mixture that detonates on impact.

**To throw a bomb:**
- Make a thrown-weapon attack roll vs. AC 10 (i.e. against a notional point in space — bombs target a square, not a creature).
- Range: 30 ft / 60 ft / 90 ft (short / medium / long).
- A miss scatters 1d6 ft × range increment in a random direction (1d8 for direction).
- Direct hit on the targeted square: full damage to the primary target, splash to anyone within 5 ft.
- Splash damage: half (rounded down), save vs. breath weapon for half again.
- The alchemist's INT bonus adds to bomb damage (not to hit).

**Base bomb damage by level:**

| Alchemist level | Damage (direct) |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | 1d6 |
| 3–4 | 2d6 |
| 5–6 | 3d6 |
| 7–8 | 4d6 |
| 9–10 | 5d6 |
| 11–12 | 6d6 |
| 13+ | +1d6 every 2 levels |

**Default bomb type is fire** (acid is common too — alchemist chooses at 1st level, can vary by bomb prepared at 5th+). Fire bombs ignite oil, paper, and dry cloth on a direct hit. Acid bombs etch metal and stone but don't ignite.

**Special bomb types** unlock as **discoveries** (see below). At 5th level and beyond, the alchemist may prepare some bombs of one type and some of another in the day's batch.

**Volatility.** If the alchemist takes 10+ damage from fire in a single hit while carrying 3+ bombs, save vs. breath weapon. Failure means one bomb cooks off in their pack (random direction, alchemist takes splash damage). This is the "alchemist explodes" rule everyone fears; the alchemist mitigates it by not standing in fireballs and by storing bombs in the kit's lined compartments.

## Salves

Salves are slow-acting unguents — applied to a body, a weapon, or a surface, and active for a short duration. They are not thrown.

### Sample Salves

- **Cure Salve.** Applied to wounds, takes one round, restores 1d8+level hp. Single use.
- **Antitoxin.** Applied to a poisoned creature; grants +4 to next save vs. that poison, or removes mild poison outright. Single use.
- **Weapon Oil — Fire.** Applied to a weapon, takes one round; weapon deals +1d4 fire damage on each hit for 5 rounds.
- **Weapon Oil — Bane.** Choose one creature type at brewing (undead, demon, lycanthrope, etc.); +2 to hit and +2 damage against that type for 5 rounds.
- **Adhesive.** Applied to a surface (door, lock, foot); next creature to touch it sticks until they tear free with a STR check vs. 18.
- **Numbing Cream.** Applied to a recipient; next save vs. pain, fear, or torture is at +4. The recipient also takes half damage from the next physical hit (the body simply doesn't register it). Lasts 1 turn.
- **Smoke Pellet.** Crushed underfoot or thrown; obscuring cloud 10 ft radius for 3 rounds. Concealment for everyone inside.

## Mutagens (Optional Subsystem)

A mutagen is a dangerous self-only brew that temporarily reshapes the alchemist's biochemistry. Mutagens are **not** counted against extracts/day; they're a separate slot.

- **Number per day:** 1 at 4th level, 2 at 8th, 3 at 12th.
- **Effect:** Drinker gains +4 to one chosen physical stat (STR, DEX, or CON) and +2 natural AC, but suffers −2 to one chosen mental stat (INT, WIS, or CHA), for 1 hour per alchemist level.
- **Restriction:** Only the alchemist who brewed the mutagen can drink it without harm. Any other drinker takes 2d6 damage and saves vs. poison or is sickened (−2 to all rolls) for 1 hour.
- **Stack limit:** One mutagen active at a time. Drinking a second before the first ends causes the alchemist to overdose (4d6 damage, save vs. death or unconscious for 1 hour).

If you want a leaner class, drop mutagens entirely. They turn the alchemist into a half-melee class; without them, the alchemist is purely a back-rank thrower-and-supporter.

## Discoveries

Every two levels (2, 4, 6, 8, ...), the alchemist learns a **discovery** — a permanent class feature, often a new bomb type or a refinement of existing brewing.

### Sample Discoveries

- **Acid Bomb.** Bombs deal acid damage. Splash continues for 1d3 rounds at 1d6 per round. Bypasses fire resistance.
- **Concussive Bomb.** Replace fire damage with thunder. Direct hit target saves vs. paralysis or stunned 1 round. Splash victims save vs. paralysis or deafened 1 turn.
- **Smoke Bomb.** No damage; creates a 20-ft-radius obscuring cloud for 1 turn. Counts as a bomb prep slot.
- **Sticky Bomb.** Splash damage repeats one extra round (the goo keeps burning/eating).
- **Sleep Bomb.** No damage; all creatures within splash radius save vs. spell or fall asleep for 1d6 turns. No level cap.
- **Holy Water Bomb.** Bomb fluid is consecrated. Damage applies as positive energy (full to undead/demons, ineffective vs. living). Direct hit on undead: 4d4 instead of normal dice.
- **Healing Bomb.** Bomb heals instead of damages. Splash radius gains hp equal to the bomb's normal damage. Costs 2 bomb slots.
- **Fast Hands.** Once per round, the alchemist may throw a second bomb at −2 to hit (off-hand penalty).
- **Precise Bombs.** The alchemist may exclude up to (INT bonus) creatures from the splash radius. Allies in the blast no longer take damage.
- **Explosive Bomb.** Splash radius increases to 10 ft. Direct hit damage unchanged.
- **Extend Extract.** One extract per day has its duration doubled.
- **Combine Extracts.** Once per day, the alchemist may drink two extracts as a single action; both take effect.
- **Mutagen Mastery.** Mutagen bonus becomes +6/−3 instead of +4/−2.
- **Lingering Salve.** Salves applied to others remain potent for 24 hours after application instead of being consumed at the moment of effect.
- **Tinker's Eye.** The alchemist gains +2 to checks involving locks, traps, and mechanical devices, and may *detect magic* once per day at will.

The list is open-ended; treat it like the wizard's spell list rather than a fixed menu. New discoveries can be invented for the campaign, found in other alchemists' notebooks, or rewarded for completing alchemy-themed quests.

## Formula Book

The alchemist's parallel to a spellbook. It contains every formula the alchemist has ever learned. Each morning's brewing is prepared from this book.

- **Starting formulas.** All 1st-rank formulas in the campaign's standard set (roughly the sample list above), plus 2 of the alchemist's choice from any rank they can prepare.
- **Per-level addition.** Two new formulas of any rank the alchemist can prepare are added to the book on each level-up. These represent the alchemist's own research.
- **Copying formulas.** A formula found in another alchemist's notebook can be transcribed into the witch's, taking 1 day per rank and 100 gp per rank in materials.
- **Gnome bonus.** Gnomish alchemists add 3 formulas per level instead of 2, and may transcribe formulas at half cost.

**If the formula book is destroyed**, the alchemist retains all known discoveries (those are skill, not notes) and may continue to brew their **starting** formulas at 1st rank only, until the book is rewritten. Rewriting takes 2 weeks per rank lost and 200 gp per rank in ingredients. Lost formulas above 1st rank must be rediscovered or recopied.

## Lab

The alchemist's lab is distinct from the kit. The kit (carried) is for daily brewing. The lab (stationary, in a town or stronghold) is for **research**: discovering new formulas, copying captured notebooks, brewing one-off magic items.

- **Cost to establish a lab:** 1,000 gp at 1st level (a basic setup); 5,000 gp at 5th (proper alchemical bench); 20,000 gp at 9th (full establishment with ventilation, fume hoods, and exotic apparatus).
- **What a lab enables:** copying formulas, brewing potions of permanent effect (e.g. *potion of heroism*, *philtre of love*), researching campaign-specific bombs and salves.
- A 9th-level alchemist with a full lab and downtime is the campaign's go-to source for permanent potions — far cheaper than asking a wizard.

## Restrictions and Drawbacks

- **No spellcasting.** The alchemist is not a caster. *Counterspell*, *anti-magic shell*, and similar effects do not interfere with extracts, bombs, or salves — those are chemistry, not magic. (DM's call: many groups rule that *anti-magic* shuts down magical effects regardless of source. Make this explicit at session zero.)
- **No turning undead, no holy symbols, no priestly access.** The alchemist's healing salves do not require divine magic and so cannot be turned off by an evil temple, but they also cannot, e.g. raise the dead.
- **Brewing tax.** One hour every dawn of brewing time. If the dawn is interrupted (party attacked at first light, urgent travel before dawn), the alchemist gets no batch that day.
- **Volatility.** As above — the alchemist near fire is the alchemist's nightmare.
- **Reaction Penalty.** In rural or superstitious areas, alchemists are routinely confused with poisoners, witches, or necromancers. −1 to −2 reaction in such places. In cities and dwarven holds, no penalty (and often a bonus among gnomes).
- **Cannot multi-class with priest.** The class is materialist by definition; the disciplines exclude one another. Multi-class with mage or thief is allowed and works well (gnome alchemist/illusionist is a strong combo).
