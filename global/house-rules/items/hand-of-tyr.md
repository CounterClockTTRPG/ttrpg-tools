# Hand of Tyr (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A great sword of severe, unornamented make, named for the one-handed god of just war and lawful oaths. It is balanced so finely that, despite its size, it may be wielded one-handed as a longsword by anyone strong enough to lift it — a feat that strikes most who try it as faintly miraculous. There is no jewel-work, no glow, no flourish; the Hand of Tyr is a weapon for those who keep their word, and it announces this with nothing but its weight and its silence.

It is intensely opinionated about how it is used. Wielders describe it as honest to the point of inconvenience: a blade that will gladly cleave a foe met fairly and will turn to lead in the hand the instant its bearer reaches for an unfair advantage. It has no patience at all for the dishonourable, and a famously short memory for second chances.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A plain, oversized two-handed sword of fine balance. *Detect Magic* shows a strong aura; *identify* reveals the +2 enchantment and the one-handed property, while the full strictures and the sword's will are named by *legend lore*.
- **Intelligence.** Int 6, Ego 26, alignment Lawful Good. Communicates by empathy and forceful impression — it cannot speak, but its judgement is unmistakable.
- **Enchantment.** +2 great sword. By virtue of its balance it may be wielded one-handed and used in all respects as a longsword (same attacks, same speed) by a wielder who meets the great sword's normal Strength requirement.
- **Alignment lock.** Only a wielder of Good alignment, acting under honourable intentions, may use it. A non-Good creature who grips it gains no benefit and feels its hostility at once.
- **Honourable combat only.** The sword permits itself to be used only in honourable combat. If it detects dishonourable intent in its wielder — striking a yielding foe, a treacherous blow, an attack on the helpless or the unaware for gain — it forces itself *to the ground*: the wielder cannot raise or swing it for that action, and it may wrench free of the grip entirely.
- **Riddance.** A wielder who repeatedly attempts to use the sword dishonourably is abandoned by it. Given its Ego of 26, the sword effectively always wins this contest of wills; it will see itself lost, dropped at a critical moment, or otherwise removed from an owner who persists in misusing it.
- **Limitations.** No other powers — no light, no detection, no special damage. Its sole genius is the one-handed great-sword wielding; everything else about it is judgement and restraint.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. A +2 great sword usable one-handed as a longsword is a superb fighting weapon, worth perhaps 12,000–16,000 gp — but only to the right buyer. Its towering Ego and strict honour-lock make it useless and even dangerous to anyone not of Good alignment, so its practical market is narrow: paladins, honourable fighters, and crusading clerics who can meet its standards and will welcome them.

## DM Notes

The tension here is the leash. The mechanical prize — a great sword's damage at a longsword's grip and speed, plus +2 — is excellent, and the price of admission is behaving with honour at all times, which is the part players will test. Decide in advance what *this* table counts as dishonourable: the source names treachery and refusing fair combat, so treat coup-de-grace on the helpless, backstabbing a parley, and striking a foe who has yielded as the clear triggers, and adjudicate murkier cases (ambush of a known villain, lethal force against bandits) in the wielder's favour unless intent is plainly foul.

With Ego 26 the sword meaningfully outranks most wielders in a personality conflict, so resolve persistent misuse narratively rather than by repeated dice — one warning by dropping to the ground, then desertion. Played straight it is less a magic item than a covenant: the blade is the reward for being the kind of character who deserves it.
