# The Eye of Iaxolok (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A smooth oval of cloudy quartz, no larger than a real eye and, to the untrained appraiser, worth no more than a handful of coins. Its modest look is the point: the Eye holds no reservoir of magic of its own. It is one of those rare focusing items that supplies no power, only a shape for power to take — the owner casts a spell *through* it, and the Eye converts that spell's energy into an effect of its own choosing. The level of the spell spent decides which effect answers.

When first found the quartz tends toward near-transparency. Carry it for half a day and it begins, faintly, to wake. Its true use is darker: a wizard who sets the stone into an empty eye socket finds it grafts to the skull on the instant, and it is said more than one prior owner put out a good eye to make the room.

The archmage Iaxolok fashioned it after losing his own left eye to the green dragon Vaprithax — a replacement that saw, and did rather more besides.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A cloudy quartz oval, unremarkable to the eye. *Detect Magic* shows a faint aura; *Identify* or *legend lore* names it a magic-focusing item and reveals the graft requirement and the table of effects.
- **Attunement.** Held, worn, or carried by one character for 12 consecutive hours, the Eye grants a +1 bonus to saving throws. Passed to a new owner, it goes dormant and must re-attune over another 12 hours.
- **Grafting.** A magic-user or wizard who sets the stone into an empty socket fuses it permanently. Only a *wish* or the owner's death separates them. Once grafted: the +1 save bonus versus magic becomes permanent, and the owner sees through the Eye as a normal eye.
- **Channeling.** The grafted caster may spend a memorized spell through the Eye to trigger one effect, keyed to that spell's level. The Eye neither stores nor combines levels — a 1st-level spell yields only the 1st-level effect, and so on. **I:** night vision (as a cat or goblin) to 60 feet for 1 hour. **II:** see traps, as *find traps*, for 3 turns. **III:** a gaze that produces a *charm person* effect. **IV:** *Shimmerflash* — 1d10+10 darting motes of light beset one target, imposing a -3 penalty to AC and attack rolls (reduced to -1 on a successful save vs. spell); a target attempting to cast must first make a Wisdom check or the spell is disrupted. **V:** a gaze that produces a *flesh to stone* effect.
- **Limitations.** The Eye is inert to non-spellcasters beyond the save bonus. Each channeling carries a non-cumulative 2% chance per spell level of draining 1 point of Constitution from the owner (a 3rd-level spell: 6%).

## Rarity and Value

Very rare. Perhaps 9,000–15,000 gp to a wizard willing to pay the price of admission, though its worth is largely academic — the only owners who profit by it are those prepared to lose an eye, and a point of Constitution now and again. Collectors prize the ungrafted stone; practitioners covet what it becomes.

## DM Notes

The tension is the bargain. The Eye is genuinely powerful in a grafted wizard's hands, but every use of the gaze effects costs a spell *and* gambles with Constitution, and the graft is effectively irreversible short of a *wish*. Let players feel the weight of that one-way door before they cut.

The original is silent on whether the gaze effects allow saves: rule them as the named spells do (*charm person* and *flesh to stone* each permit save vs. spell), with the gaze acting as the delivery rather than a new attack. Constitution loss is permanent unless restored by *restoration* or similar; track it, because a caster who leans on the 5th-level effect will whittle himself down fast — which is exactly the leash Iaxolok built in.
