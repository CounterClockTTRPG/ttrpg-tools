# Ring of Flickering (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A plain band that, when worn and triggered, makes its bearer *stutter* in and out of the world. One instant they stand where they stood; the next they are simply not there; the next they are back — a strobe of presence and absence that lasts a short while and unsettles everyone watching. To an onlooker the bearer seems to blink like a guttering candle, never quite gone and never quite reliably present.

Where the bearer goes in the gaps depends on the ring. Most send the wearer a hair's breadth out of phase; others flick them to a fixed site on the Prime set by the maker, or to another plane or demiplane entirely, or briefly into the grip of an *imprisonment*. The pattern of the flicker, too, varies from ring to ring. The makers, whoever they were, clearly prized misdirection over comfort.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** An unadorned ring. *Detect Magic* shows an alteration aura; *identify* or *legend lore* reveals the flicker effect, the per-day limit, and the ring's flicker pattern, but not always its destination.
- **Activation.** Usable 3 times per day. When triggered, the bearer enters a flicker cycle lasting 2 full rounds (20 segments). A round in 2e is 10 segments; on the segment of activation the bearer vanishes, on the next segment reappears, and so alternates — absent on odd segments, present on even, for the cycle's duration.
- **Initiative.** The bearer still rolls and acts within the normal initiative order, but may take actions only on segments when present. On vanished segments no action is possible. Resolve a flickering combatant segment by segment against the round's segment count.
- **What goes with them.** All worn and carried personal possessions vanish with the bearer. Other creatures and large objects do not. Any ongoing effect the bearer generates (e.g. a *protection from evil, 10' radius*) departs with them while vanished and resumes at the point of disappearance when they return.
- **While vanished.** The bearer cannot act on or influence the place they left, but remains aware of all movement within their normal sensory range there — they are not blinded to what happens during their absence.
- **Dodging.** Vanishing on a given segment lets the bearer evade any effect that would strike them *that segment only*. An effect persisting longer than a segment still catches them when they reappear.
- **Time passes normally.** Vanished segments count as elapsed time for spell durations, regeneration, poison onset, and the like.
- **Spellcasting.** The interruptions disrupt casting; the ring is rarely triggered when the bearer intends long casting times, as flicker segments break concentration.
- **Negation and traps.** The ring resists *dispel magic* but yields to higher-order negations (*disjunction* and the like). A successful deactivation returns the bearer to their point of departure and expends that use. If the departure point has become solid in the interim (e.g. a *wall of stone* raised across it), the bearer is trapped in the destination state or location until the obstruction is removed; if it is not cleared by the cycle's end, the bearer may be stranded there permanently.
- **Varieties.** Patterns differ — gone 1 / back 2, gone 5 / back 5, and more capricious rings that roll a random pattern each use. Destination likewise varies (phase, fixed Prime site, plane, demiplane, or temporary *imprisonment*).
- **Limitations.** No save accompanies ordinary use; the danger is self-inflicted — a blocked return point or an unfortunate destination.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. Worth 6,000–12,000 gp to assassins, duelists, and infiltrators who value the ability to flicker out of a killing blow's path. The wall-of-stone failure mode and the alien-plane variants depress the price among the cautious; reckless buyers pay full freight.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension is that flickering is both an escape and a gamble. Run it strictly segment by segment in combat — the bearer is a maddening target, immune on half the count yet equally unable to act then. Adjudicate "dodging" narrowly: only instantaneous, single-segment effects are evaded; lingering hazards (a *flaming sphere*, a gas cloud, a grapple) simply wait for the bearer's return.

The return-point trap is the ring's teeth. A clever enemy who realizes what the bearer is wearing can wall off the spot and strand them. When the destination is another plane, treat a permanent stranding as a genuine campaign event, not a footnote — that risk is the price of the power, and players should be allowed to learn the pattern of their ring before they stake their lives on it.
