# Malhrek's Staff of Flame (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A gnarled shaft of dark redwood, knotted and root-twisted along its length, crowned with a single red crystal whose origin no sage has ever named. It is a serviceable weapon in its own right, but its true purpose is subtler: it is a *magic-focusing item*. Such objects hold no charges and supply no power of their own — they contain only enough enchantment to take the wielder's own spell energy and bend it into a new form. The caster casts their own spells *through* the staff, and what emerges is fire.

The staff was the preferred weapon of Malhrek, the Ancient Grethian Wizard-King, who bound into the crystal a fire elemental of 12 Hit Dice and tricked it into focusing spell energy on his behalf for an age. The elemental labours still, holding the crystal's magical balance steady — and woe to anyone who breaks the bargain it does not know it is keeping.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A twisted redwood staff topped with an unidentifiable red crystal. *Detect Magic* shows a strong evocation aura and a bound elemental presence within the crystal; *identify* or *legend lore* names it as a focusing item and reveals the level table below.
- **Weapon.** Functions as a staff of +2 enchantment, granting +2 to attack and damage rolls in melee.
- **Focusing.** A magic-user or wizard may cast their own memorized spells through the staff. The staff converts each spell into a fire effect keyed to the spell's *level* — it cannot store or convert spell levels, so a 1st-level spell always produces the level I effect, a 2nd-level spell the level II effect, and so on. The spell slot is expended normally.
  - **Level I:** *affect normal fires* or *burning hands* (caster's choice)
  - **Level II:** *flaming sphere* or *pyrotechnics* (caster's choice)
  - **Level III:** *flame arrow* or *Melf's minute meteors* (caster's choice)
  - **Level IV:** *wall of fire*
  - **Level V:** a pyrotechnic burst equivalent to the priest's *flame strike*
- **The bound elemental.** The 12-HD fire elemental within the crystal focuses each spell *before* it can disturb the crystal's fixed magical balance. The crystal is impervious to nearly all damage. The shaft is not.
- **Limitations.** Spells of 6th level and higher cannot be focused (no effect exists for them); the staff merely fails and the slot is wasted. Only an arcane caster can use the focusing property. The staff does not enhance, extend, or empower the original spell in any way beyond its conversion to the listed fire effect.

## Rarity and Value

Very rare. Worth 25,000–40,000 gp to an evoker or war-wizard who wants a reliable fire arsenal without memorizing fire spells — and who is willing to carry a bound elemental on a stick. The unique provenance and the catastrophe hook make most cautious buyers hesitate.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension is that the staff turns a wizard's whole spellbook into graded fire, trading flexibility for thematic reliability — and that its destruction is a campaign event. If the shaft is ever broken, the 12-HD elemental is released in an insane rage and will slay every living thing in the area. Treat this as a genuine threat to the party, not a slap on the wrist: it is the price of mishandling a Wizard-King's relic.

Adjudicate the "overload" carefully. The elemental's whole job is to keep the crystal in balance, so ordinary use never overloads it — the danger is purely physical breakage of the redwood shaft. Where players try to exploit the +2/level-I combo for cheap, note that they still expend real spell slots and gain no level scaling, so the effects stay modest. The staff rewards a fire specialist and quietly punishes anyone who treats it as disposable.
