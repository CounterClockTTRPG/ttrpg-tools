# Backhand (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A cloth patch about twice the size of a man's hand, edged in gold braid around a centre of black velvet, worked in the shape of an open palm. It is meant to be sewn or otherwise bonded to a garment — a cloak, a jerkin, the back of a coat — with the palm facing rearward, toward the very angle from which a knife in the dark tends to come. Stitched in place it lies flat and lifeless, an odd bit of finery and nothing more.

It wakes only at the worst possible moment for whoever triggers it. The instant a blade finds the wearer's back, a glowing fist erupts from the velvet, seizes the attacker's hand or wrist, and turns their own lunge against them — hauling them in, twisting the weapon about, and burying it in the body that meant to wield it.

Such patches are favoured by those who have already been stabbed in the back once and did not enjoy the lesson.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** An embroidered hand-shaped patch, gold braid on black velvet. *Detect Magic* reveals a faint abjuration aura; *Identify* names the property and the backstab trigger.
- **Installation.** Must be sewn or bonded to a worn garment, palm facing rearward, to function. One patch protects one wearer; the patch is inert in a pack or pocket.
- **Trigger.** The patch activates only against a *successful* backstab — the rogue's positional attack that earns the damage multiplier. Ordinary rear or flanking attacks do not set it off.
- **Effect.** On a triggered backstab, the attacker is seized and their own momentum reversed. The blow is turned back: the attacker suffers the damage (and any rider effects — poison, magical weapon properties, sneak-attack multiplier) that would have struck the wearer. The wearer takes none of it.
- **Limitations.** Functions against melee backstabs delivered with a held weapon that can be turned; it does nothing against ranged backstabs (a bow or thrown dagger leaves no wrist to catch) or against attacks that are not true backstabs. The fist catches one attacker per triggering blow.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. Roughly 4,000–7,000 gp, and worth every coin to the paranoid — wealthy merchants, faction lieutenants, anyone whose enemies prefer the quiet kind of murder. Its value is wasted on a front-line warrior and priceless to a man who fears the corridor behind him.

## DM Notes

The interesting tension is that Backhand turns the assassin's greatest strength into their greatest liability: a backstab is high-multiplier damage, and reflecting it can drop the killer with their own opening strike. That makes it a hard counter to a single dangerous knife — and useless against a swarm, which is the balance.

Adjudicate "would have struck the wearer" generously: roll the attacker's backstab damage as normal, then apply it to the attacker instead of the target. The original is silent on repeat use; rule it triggers every time the condition is met, with no daily limit, since the cost is borne entirely by whoever is foolish enough to keep stabbing.
