# Skull Leech (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A long sword of pale mithral, its blade unusually slender, that drinks a bright green light from the air the moment a living hand closes on the grip — a sickly emerald glow that fills a fifteen-foot radius and will not be hidden. The name is older than any record of the weapon, and it is honest: the sword feeds, on its enemies and, if not fed, on the one who carries it.

It is said to have crossed half the known lands at the hip of a witch of fearsome reputation, who suffered its daily appetite for the wounds it let her shrug off in return. The sword was stolen from her some years ago and has surfaced and vanished in rumour several times since. Those who have held it describe the same thing: a faint, constant pull, as though the grip itself were thirsty.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A slim mithral long sword. *Detect Magic* shows a strong necromantic aura; *identify* reveals the combat bonuses, the on-hit drain, and the daily toll. The green glow appears only when the sword is held by a living creature.
- **Enchantment.** +3 to hit and +2 to damage.
- **Life drain on hit.** Each successful hit transfers 2 hp from the target to the wielder. These hp may carry the wielder *above* their normal maximum, where they function as a temporary reserve the sword holds against future wounds (see below). Undead, constructs, and other creatures without life force yield nothing.
- **The daily toll.** The sword draws 2 hp from its wielder every day. This loss can reduce the wielder only to unconsciousness, never to death — it stops at 1 hp. The toll can be paid down only by the sword's own stored reserve, or recovered by a full eight hours' rest.
- **Stored healing.** Hit-point gains from the drain that exceed the wielder's maximum are banked by the sword. The wielder may draw on this reserve to heal real wounds, or it is spent automatically to cover the daily toll. The DM tracks the reserve; it is not unlimited and does not persist if the wielder dies or relinquishes the blade.
- **The glow.** A bright green light, 15-foot radius, while held by a living wielder. It cannot be suppressed short of sheathing the sword or setting it down.
- **Limitations.** The drain functions only on melee hits against living creatures. The glow forbids stealth. The daily toll applies whether or not the sword is used in combat, so long as it is carried.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. Worth roughly 9,000–14,000 gp to someone who can stomach the upkeep — typically a frontline fighter or a darker spellsword who values the self-healing in a long delve. The constant glow, the necromantic taint, and the daily hit-point cost keep cautious buyers away, which is part of why it keeps changing hands.

## DM Notes

The tension is the ledger. Skull Leech is excellent in sustained fights — every hit tops the wielder back up, and surplus banks against the next bad day — but it taxes its bearer between fights, when there is nothing to feed it. A party that delves hard and rests rarely will find the daily 2 hp a real and accumulating drain; a wielder who sheathes it for days at a time should be reminded it still feeds as long as it is carried (rule that simply carrying it counts; only being truly parted from it stops the toll).

Watch the over-maximum reserve so it does not become an infinite battery — cap it at a sensible ceiling (the wielder's normal maximum is a clean choice) and let it bleed away if unused over a few days. The "unconsciousness, never death" floor is deliberate: the sword is a parasite, not an assassin. It keeps its host alive precisely so it can keep feeding.
