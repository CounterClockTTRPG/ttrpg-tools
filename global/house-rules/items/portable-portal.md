# Portable Portal (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A short rod of black metal, roughly an inch across and four inches long, smooth and polished to a hard shine. Close inspection finds a fine seam running the circumference at its middle. Twist the two ends a half-turn against each other, give a slight jerk, and the rod parts in two. One half — call it Piece A — shows a smooth concave white face. The other, Piece B, bears a small dull-grey hemispherical button. Piece A is meant to be set down and left in an open spot; Piece B goes in the owner's pocket and travels.

Press the button on Piece B and a glowing blue ovoid swells into being, looking for all the world like solid glass — until a hand meets it and finds warm water, yielding and bottomless. Step in from any side and step out of a matching ovoid wherever Piece A was left, or pass the other way, from A to B. It is a door folded into a pocket, and like any door it can be walked through by people you did not invite.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A black metal rod with a faint central seam; twisting it apart yields concave Piece A and buttoned Piece B. *Detect Magic* shows a strong conjuration/alteration aura; *Identify* reveals the two-piece operation and the closing hazard.
- **Activation.** Pressing the button on Piece B opens the portal before the holder, linked to wherever Piece A rests. The portal stays open for 1 turn.
- **Throughput.** One creature of man-size or smaller may pass each round, in either direction, for the duration. Pursuers may follow through freely — it does not discriminate.
- **Closing.** When the turn ends, both ovoids shrink and dim, fading over 1 round. Any creature entering during this closing round must save vs. death or be lost forever in the vortices of nil space; those who save are cast onto the Astral Plane, their bodies left unconscious where the portal stood. Once gone, the two pieces reappear, reconnected, at Piece A's location.
- **Displaced Piece A.** Anyone may pick up and move an unattended Piece A. If it lies somewhere a man cannot stand (stowed in a chest, a tight crevice), travellers arriving there must save vs. death or be lost in nil space; on a success they arrive exhausted and in dire need of rest, as near to Piece A as can be managed.
- **Limitations.** Same-plane operation only, as far as is known. The portal is indiscriminate (foes follow allies) and the closing window is lethal — the device punishes the late and the careless.

## Rarity and Value

Rare, and dearer in pairs. A single rod runs perhaps 12,000–18,000 gp; a matched pair, affording true two-way transit, is rarer still and often priced beyond reason. Coveted by adventurers, smugglers, and any wizard who would rather cache treasure at home than haul it through a dungeon.

## DM Notes

The Portal's tension is the trade between convenience and risk. Used wisely — Piece A hidden somewhere safe and familiar, a forest near home or a sealed vault — it lets a party of up to ten ferry loot, restock, heal, and study between forays. Used in a panic as an escape hatch, it cheerfully admits the very things being fled, and the 1-round closing window turns a botched retreat into a save-or-die.

Run the closing hazard strictly; it is the item's whole leash. Track who has crossed each round so the tenth straggler feels the squeeze. The displaced-Piece-A rule is your lever against trivial home-base abuse — a clever rival or a curious goblin who pockets the little white disc can strand the party, which is a fine adventure in its own right.
