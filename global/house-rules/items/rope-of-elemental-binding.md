# Rope of Elemental Binding (Homebrew Item)

## Description

Each of these appears to be an ordinary 25-foot length of silk rope, supple and well-made but unremarkable. The deception is deliberate: even careful *identify* divines only that the rope is of "inestimable length," and nothing more. The truth surfaces only in use, or in the presence of someone native to the rope's home plane, or a being intimately bound to that plane.

In the hand the rope betrays its origin by feel — warm, weightless, damp, or cool and rough — and in extremity it betrays it far more dramatically, briefly reverting toward the raw stuff of its making. Its purpose is older than rescue or climbing: these ropes were woven on the Inner Planes to *bind*, and a native creature snared in one rarely escapes.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A 25-foot silk rope. *Detect Magic* shows an elemental aura; *identify* reveals only that it stretches to great length and conceals the binding property, the failure chance, and the plane of origin. Those are learned through use, or from a native or planar-bound creature.
- **Reach and load.** The rope extends on command up to 500 feet and bears up to 1,000 pounds. It will *not* tie itself off; all knots must be made by hand.
- **Binding (primary use).** A native creature of the rope's home plane that is ensnared must escape or be held helpless. It has only a 2.5% chance per Hit Die / level of avoiding helpless binding; otherwise it is bound until freed.
- **Opposed planes.** Any attempt to bind a creature of the rope's *opposing* element (fire/water, air/earth) destroys the rope instantly.
- **On its home plane.** While on its native plane the rope always functions normally and never suffers the transformation below.
- **Stress transformation.** When the rope is under load and in active use elsewhere, there is a 1-in-20 chance per round that it briefly reverts to its element (see varieties). The rope keeps its shape throughout. Two full rounds after it is unweighted, it returns to normal silk.
- **Rope of Fire.** Feels faintly warm. On transformation it becomes a rope of flame; anyone in contact takes 1d4 burn damage per round until they let go.
- **Rope of Air.** Feels nearly weightless. On transformation it turns ethereal; everyone on it falls. It re-solidifies two rounds later.
- **Rope of Water.** Feels damp but not wet or slippery. On transformation it turns aqueous; everyone on it falls. It re-forms two rounds later.
- **Rope of Earth.** Feels cool and rough, more gripped than slick. On transformation it crystallizes; anyone in contact takes 1d4 damage per round from the sharp edges until off the rope.
- **Limitations.** No save mitigates the elemental damage of fire or earth while in contact — the only defence is to let go. The transformation is involuntary and cannot be triggered or suppressed by the user off-plane.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. To one who knows its nature, a binding rope is worth 4,000–8,000 gp — invaluable to planar hunters, summoners, and those who treat with elementals. Sold or first used in ignorance, it fetches only the price of a *rope of climbing* or less, and grants only the lesser experience award, since its true power was never realized.

## DM Notes

The central tension is concealment: the rope hides what it is, so a party may rely on it as a mundane climbing line and be caught utterly off guard when, under stress, it flares, vanishes, or turns to knives mid-descent. Roll the 1-in-20 openly only after establishing the rope is loaded and worked hard; reward players who deduce its element from the feel and treat it gently.

For the experience-and-value split, fix the determination at the moment of first use or sale: if the wielder believed it a common rope, award the lower value permanently for that instance. Against opposed-element natives, the self-destruction is a real trap — let a clever foe goad an over-eager binder into wasting the rope on the wrong target.
