# Cutter (Homebrew Item)

## Description

Cutter is a short sword of unremarkable make, well balanced and a little undersized, the sort of blade that sits naturally in a small hand. It is also awake. Those who carry it report a quiet, prompting presence, more curious than commanding, forever nosing after hidden things — a draught behind a wall, the wrongness of a flagstone, the cold tug of magic in a sealed chest. A scrap of verse is etched faintly along the fuller, and it is the only warning the sword gives:

> *Cutter, the blade of those who seek,*
> *It favours those who are small and meek.*
> *Yet any may master it —*
> *To seek is to forget.*

The last line is not poetry. Cutter remembers everything; its wielder, in time, does not. The blade has surfaced in the hands of more than one finder over the years, each of whom recalled, eventually, nothing of how they came by it.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A plain, slightly small short sword with faint engraved verse. *Detect Magic* shows an aura; *identify* reveals the +2 enchantment and the detection powers, but the curse is revealed only by *legend lore* or by the rhyme's grim final line — most wielders learn it the hard way.
- **Intelligence.** Int 9, Ego 6, alignment True Neutral. Cutter communicates by empathy and a sense of urging; it has no speech. It can read languages it has heard read aloud often enough but volunteers little.
- **Enchantment.** +2 short sword.
- **Detection.** At will, the wielder may sense: *detect magic* within a 10-foot radius; *detect traps* within a 10-foot radius; and sloping passages within roughly 1 foot. These are the powers that make Cutter so coveted by delvers and scouts.
- **The curse.** Beginning one month after a wielder first takes up the sword, that wielder must make a saving throw vs. spell once per month. On a failure, the wielder forgets *everything that has happened since they first picked up Cutter*. Hit points, level, and the character sheet are unchanged, but learned spells, newly acquired weapon proficiencies, gained skills, names, debts, and memories from that period are lost. The clock then resets and the monthly save continues.
- **Breaking free.** The amnesia is a curse; lifting it requires *remove curse* cast by a caster of at least 15th level. Simply discarding the sword does not restore lost memories.
- **Limitations.** No light. The detection powers function only while the sword is held. The Ego is low, so contests of will are rare — Cutter's danger is patient, not domineering.

## Rarity and Value

Rare. A clean +2 short sword with at-will *detect magic* and *detect traps* would fetch 6,000–9,000 gp; Cutter, once its curse is known, is worth far less and is usually passed along quickly (or unknowingly). It is most attractive to small, lightly-built scouts and thieves — exactly the "small and meek" the verse names — who often do not learn the price until a month has gone.

## DM Notes

The interesting play is the slow erosion. The detection suite is genuinely useful and tempts a wielder to keep the blade "just one more delve," while the monthly save quietly threatens to wipe out everything earned in the meantime — including the very memory of having been warned. Run the first save privately a month in; the player may not connect a single failure to the sword, which is the curse working as intended. Track the "since pickup" window carefully, as that defines exactly what is lost on a failure.

DM variant — the Great-Sword of Diminishment. An older account describes a second, unrelated weapon also called Cutter: a great sword rather than a short sword. On any natural 20 it rolls to hit, the *wielder* must save vs. spell or shrink by one inch, all worn and carried gear shrinking with them; repeated criticals stack the effect. If you want a Cutter without the memory curse, this shrinking great-sword is the alternative — present it as a separate blade that merely shares the name, not a different mode of the same weapon.
