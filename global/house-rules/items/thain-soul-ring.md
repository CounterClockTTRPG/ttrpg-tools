# The Thain Soul Ring (Homebrew Item)

## Description

A seamless ring of a strange substance — as hard as tempered steel, yet as clear as flawless glass, so that it seems made of frozen water that never melts. Worn unawakened, it is merely a curiosity, cold and inert, and an appraiser would call it fine glasswork and nothing more.

Its true nature wakes only through sacrifice. When another being lays down their life freely to save the wearer's, a single grain of pure white light appears within the band and begins to drift in slow, patient circles through the clear material — the preserved soul of the one who died. It does not speak. It waits. And in time, if the bond is honoured, it asks to live again.

According to the few who have worn one, the Thain rings were never sold; they were given, and only ever once. A life for a life.

## Mechanics

- **Appearance.** A clear, glass-like band of steel-hard substance. While dormant, *Detect Magic* shows nothing; once awakened by sacrifice, it radiates a faint divination/conjuration aura and the white mote is plainly visible. *Identify* names the property only after awakening.
- **Awakening.** The ring gains power only when another creature *freely* gives its life to save the wearer's — a knowing, willing sacrifice, not an accident or a death in ordinary battle. The DM is the final judge of whether an act qualifies.
- **The summoned soul.** Once per day the wearer may call the preserved soul forth to fight for 5 rounds. It appears beside the wearer and fights exactly as it did in life — same Hit Dice, attacks, and abilities it possessed when living. It cannot be harmed and cannot be targeted, dispelled, or turned; it simply fades when the 5 rounds end.
- **Return to life.** If the wearer keeps the ring on without interruption for one full year, the soul floats free and reforms in living flesh — but as a human child of 0 level, with no memory of its former power. The ring then becomes inert glass once more.
- **Limitations.** One awakening only; once the soul departs (whether reborn or otherwise lost), the ring cannot be re-awakened. The summoned soul is bound to the wearer and cannot range far (within sight, roughly 60 feet). Removing the ring for any meaningful span resets the one-year count.

## Rarity and Value

Very rare. Effectively priceless to the wearer for whom it awakened, and worthless to anyone else — a dormant ring is glass, and an awakened one is bound to a single person and a single debt. No market exists; these change hands only as bequests or relics.

## DM Notes

The heart of this item is story, not combat utility. The five-round bodyguard is potent but rare, and the real weight is the year-long choice: keep the ring on through every adventure, and the saviour returns as a helpless child the party is now bound to protect — a debt repaid by inheriting a dependent. That is the intended tension, and it should be played as a moral and narrative beast rather than a mechanical one.

Adjudicate "freely" strictly: a paladin diving on a blade is a sacrifice; a soldier slain by bad luck is not. For the summoned soul, reuse its living stat block verbatim and let it act on the wearer's initiative. Where the original is silent on the soul's reach and on re-awakening, I have ruled it sight-bound and one-time-only; loosen either only if you want a recurring companion.
